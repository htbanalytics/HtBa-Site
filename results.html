The Lines

How do sports books determine the lines they offer? Essentially, sports books want an equal amount of betting dollars on BOTH sides of the line. When they get an equal 50/50 split they earn the most amount of money. Do they really think Team A is a 14 point favorite? No. The line is set based on what they believe they can take from the public to achieve that holy 50/50 split. These lines are NOT based on the actual probability of a team to win or by how much. That is the #1 public misconception in sports betting. Then as the public bets on these lines sportsbooks move the lines to keep pushing for that equal split. So if too much money is betting the under they drop the line so more people will be enticed to bet the overs.

The Application

We are not in the buiness of predicting winners. We are in the buiness of finding inequalities in the market and exploiting them. Where are the books getting greedy and where can we make money. We take the basic principle of winning probability and process that against the cost of betting to determine a "profitibility number". This is what drives our picks and ultimately our winning business model.
* Units is the average bet size used. Example: If your average bet size is $100 and you're profitable by 50 "units", your profit would be $5,000.
